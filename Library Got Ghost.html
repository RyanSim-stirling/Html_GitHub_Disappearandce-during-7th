SIM URBAN MYTHS
ARE YOU READY FOR A THRILL?

Library Got Ghost?

It started with a few whispers at first — students joking about the library being “too quiet.” But then... people started to notice things... A sound. A sudden shush - faint and deliberate, but it only comes when you are alone. Not in some dark corner, but in the middle of the open, well-lit space. The sound would slide right past your ear, like someone leaning in close, inches from your neck.

And then there was the feeling - a deep, gnawing unease of being watched. Not by a person, but by something you couldn’t see… only feel. It happened most often to students who found themselves alone in the library, and it got to the point that students leave their study sessions early, eyes darting, skin crawling under the
sterile glow of the fluorescent lights.

But the phones… That’s what turned whispers into full-blown rumors. Some of those students, the ones who were alone, have tried to record the sound... And they all reported the same thing: their devices would suddenly and completely die. One second, the battery was full. The next — dead flat. No warning. No glitch.
Just a black screen.

Except once. Someone managed to get a recording - just a few seconds of it. The camera's microphone picked up the sound: a soft, deliberate shush... drawn out like it was meant for them specifically. Then the footage cuts off. The screen went black and the phone dies. It wouldn’t come back to life until he stepped out of the campus.

And no one can explain it. There’s no tragic history. No haunted past. The library was new. Bright. Spotless. Modern. And maybe that’s what makes it even more of a mystery. Because sometimes, the scariest things don’t come from the past. They don’t need a legend. They just arrive — uninvited, unexplained — in the bright, ordinary places we trust the most.
